# Instagram Style References — Accounts to Emulate

Garrett has identified three trade-services Instagram accounts Anderson should emulate. This file is the agent's reference — read it before every infographic-format post and check the caption + asset against the common-pattern checklist at the bottom.

## Why these three?

All three sell residential/commercial trades services (garage doors, pest control, HVAC+plumbing), all have disciplined feeds, and all have built brand equity through **repeating visual anchors** and **named programs** rather than generic job-site photography.

---

## @a1garagedoorservice — what to steal

- **Founder-forward content**: CEO Tommy Mello appears on vans, in podcasts, in explainer content. The account has a face.
- **Named programs with dollar figures**: "Door Buyback Program — up to $1,000". Commoditizes trust with a specific offer.
- **Educational posture**: podcast clips, "why your door is broken" explainers, process walk-throughs.
- **Minimal-overlay job photography**: clean install photos; caption carries the message.
- **Sustainability hook**: trade-in framing on a commodity service. Sets them apart from local competitors.

**For Anderson**: we don't have a founder-face play (and shouldn't invent one), but the **named program with dollar figure** is the #1 borrowable move. Surface the Free Key Audit and Medeco Upgrade Program in captions + graphics, same way A1 surfaces Door Buyback.

## @greenmangopestcontrol — what to steal

- **Visually ownable gimmick**: UV-lit/blacklight scorpion content. Nobody else in pest control does this. Stops the scroll at thumbnail scale.
- **Consistent icon system**: each service category (termites, scorpions, bed bugs) has its own repeating icon treatment. Recognizable at IG grid thumbnail scale.
- **Desert/local anchor**: cactus, scorpion, Arizona motifs. Locally specific without being hokey.
- **Guarantee language**: "If pests come back, so do we." Compresses a lot of trust into one line.
- **Disciplined format split**: photography, video, and graphic tiles are distinct streams, not one mushy feed.

**For Anderson**: we can't copy the UV gimmick, but we CAN own a repeating visual anchor. The Primary Blue (`#0045DB`) photo tint + padlock-A pattern system in [style_guide.md](style_guide.md) is our Green Mango equivalent — every graphic tile carries the same blue treatment and padlock motif. Same disciplined split: photo posts are photo posts, video posts are video posts, graphic tiles are graphic tiles. Don't mix them in a single post.

## @goettlacplumbing — what to steal

- **Live mascot as narrative device**: Sadie the Wheaten terrier appears across the feed — "Sadie Reminders," "Smart Sadie Membership," Sadie-at-the-office BTS. Not a logo add-on; a permanent character.
- **Heritage framing**: "Since 1939" — classic typography, trust-first voice.
- **Educational DIY tone**: seasonal HVAC/plumbing explainers that genuinely help before pitching.
- **Proprietary named program**: "Smart Sadie Membership" ties the character to the commercial offering.

**For Anderson**: heritage framing is our natural move — 60+ years, same family, Phoenix-only. Make the "60+ years" lockup a visible recurring element on every graphic (already baked into the image_generator watermark). Not a mascot but a character — Anderson's character IS the heritage and the commercial focus.

## What NOT to copy

- **Mascots** — Goettl's Sadie works because they committed to it for years. Inventing a mascot today is cringe. Don't.
- **Founder face as content pillar** — Anderson isn't owner-led media-style. Garrett can appear occasionally, but it's not A1's every-third-post cadence.
- **UV/gimmick photography** — Green Mango's scorpion content is unique to them. The equivalent here would feel forced.
- **Discount-driven positioning** — All three accounts avoid it, and our [ai-guidelines](../anderson-lock-and-safe-ai-guidelines.md) explicitly forbid it. Never compete on price.

## Common-pattern checklist (agent runs this on every infographic-format post)

Before creating the Buffer draft, verify:

- [ ] **Thumbnail-recognizable**: At IG grid size (~150px), could someone tell this is Anderson and not a generic locksmith? The Primary Blue photo tint, padlock-A mark, or "Securing Arizona Since 1966" lockup should make it so.
- [ ] **Named program surfaced**: If the post is about key control, safe audits, or rekey planning, surface the "Free Key Audit" by name. Bold it in the graphic if applicable.
- [ ] **Benefit-led first line**: Opening caption line states a specific benefit or pain point, not "Hey everyone!" or "Did you know that...?"
- [ ] **Disciplined stream type**: If this is a graphic tile post, it's graphic-only (don't mix with photo carousels). Same for photo-only or video-only posts.
- [ ] **Commercial framing**: 95% of references should land on commercial/B2B audiences (property managers, facilities, GCs, retail multi-tenant, government, schools). Residential is the exception, not the default.
- [ ] **Specific, not generic**: Reference a real service, a real scenario, or a real piece of the source asset. "Commercial rekey for property manager" beats "We handle all your lock needs."
- [ ] **Caption + graphic reinforce each other**: Caption answers what the graphic is showing; graphic punctuates what the caption claims. Don't put the key info only in one.
